After years of living, working and loving in Oakland, our team noticed a common complaint among the diverse, yet ever growing LGBT community in Oakland. Despite the fact that every day Oakland seemed to get better, safer and open the doors of another fun place, there was no seven day a week gathering place for the LGBT community in our thriving downtown. Our team, armed with background in small business entrepreneurship, economic development and community service sought to change that.


The management team is an especially close one. Together we share a single vision: to provide a unique and entertaining experience through exceptional service to LGBT East Bay residents and their heterosexual friends.

The Team

Sean Sullivan General Director

An award-winning mixologist, Sean Sullivan is the co-founder and managing director of The Port Bar. Sullivan first worked as bartender when he was a baby gay working for his mother’s event staffing company “We Like To Party.” From her nurturing he received on the job training in bartending and managing demanding audiences under pressure and all with a smile. For over 14 years, Sullivan worked as an executive in non-profit management. He has worked professionally for LGBT serving organizations, raised millions of dollars against California’s Proposition 8 and overseen fundraising endeavors for LGBTQ+ youth. He was recognized for his work by the San Francisco SuperBowl Committee as a “Playmaker” at this year’s SuperBowl celebration. A cheerleader for the sunny side of the Bay, Sullivan is thrilled to be part of the team that has brought The Port Bar to dock in Oakland.



Richard Fuentes Vice President

Richard Fuentes serves as the brains of The Port Bar operation, it’s co-founder and moneyman. A proud first generation Mexican-American, Fuentes worked in retail management for over five years, as well as in municipal economic development for three cities including Oakland where he helped manage the city’s budget during the worst financial crisis in a generation. When not at The Port Bar, Fuentes works at BART and serves on the board of AFSCME. Fuentes was named one of 2016’s 40 under 40 by The San Francisco Business Times. Despite his many accomplishments, Fuentes is still carded for rate R movies. So now you know why we card so much for your drinks.
